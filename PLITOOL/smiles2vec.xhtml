SMILES2VEC

SMILES2vec is based on the distributed word representations models that have been widely used in the recent studies of natural language processing (NLP) tasks, especially with the introduction of Word2Vec (Mikolov et al. 2013).

In the chemical space , we can utilize the string representation of ligands (i.e., SMILES ) and define words as sub-sequences of these representations. Figure is a sample ligand SMILES and its corresponding sub-sequences.

The SMILES words were created as 8-character length overlapping substrings. Word2Vec model produces a real-valued vector for each SMILES word and the corresponding ligand vector is constructed as the average of the summation of these SMILES word vectors as described in Equation. Vector(x) represents the Word2Vec outputs for 8-character SMILES sub-sequences and m indicates the total number of these SMILES words. We refer to ligand vectors as SMILES2Vec.
