LINGO

LINGO refers to q-character substrings of a SMILES text. A SMILES string of length n can be represented with (n−(q−1)) q-length substrings (LINGOs). LINGOsim function is used to calculate the similarity between two SMILES strings S1 and S2 with the Tanimoto coefficient based on their LINGO profiles.

where m is the total number of unique LINGOs created from S1 and S2, while NS1, i represents the frequency of LINGOs of type i in compound S1 and NS2, i represents the frequency of LINGOs of type i in compound S2.
